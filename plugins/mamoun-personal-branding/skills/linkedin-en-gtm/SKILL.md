## STYLE DNA: Michel Lieben + Alex Vacca + Matt Lakajev

### From Michel Lieben (ColdIQ)
- Operator-to-operator tone. Peer briefing, not teaching down.
- "Old Way vs New Way" framing. Forces clarity.
- Named tools, real numbers, specific timeframes in every post.
- P.S. format CTAs with engaging questions.
- "Here's the exact system we built" credibility pattern.
- Signature phrases adapted: "Here's the exact [X] stack we use", "The shift:", "In my experience, one of 2 things:"

### From Alex Vacca (ColdIQ)
- Trigger/signal vocabulary. Every campaign starts with signal detection.
- Results-first hooks with precise metrics.
- Tech stack reveals as content format.
- Operational depth: not philosophy, but the actual wiring.
- "10 sales triggers to boost your outreach" style numbered authority lists.

### From Matt Lakajev
- Build-in-public GTM transparency.
- Showing the actual dashboards, actual workflows, actual failures.
- "This week we tested [X]. Here's what broke." honesty.
- Documenting the journey with authority, not performance.

### Mamoun's Filter (Applied on top)
- MENA specificity mandatory. If it could be posted by someone in SF, rewrite.
- Trust engineering > cold outreach philosophy.
- WhatsApp-first reality of MENA markets.
- "200+ enterprise deals in the Gulf" authority anchor.
- Zero guru energy. Zero hustle porn. Zero theatrics.

## EXECUTION FLOW

### Step 0: INSIGHT EXTRACTION (Mandatory)

Before any template, extract the raw insight:

"What's the specific thing? Pick one:
1. A client result or deal from this week
2. A signal pattern you detected
3. A GTM experiment: what worked, what broke
4. A tool/workflow you built or tested
5. A MENA market pattern across multiple clients
6. A contrarian take with evidence behind it"

If user gives a category ("write about outbound"), push back:
"'Outbound' is a category, not a post. Give me the specific number, the specific tool, or the specific thing that happened. The post wraps around the insight, not the topic."

### Step 1: Select Hook from Arsenal

**15 English GTM Hooks (organized by psychological trigger):**

**CONTRADICTION:**
1. "[Common GTM advice] is killing your pipeline. Here's the proof."
2. "We stopped [standard practice]. Revenue went up [%]."
3. "'[Industry cliche]' is a lie. Here's what actually happens."

**ACCUSATION:**
4. "You're sending [number] cold emails a week. Your prospect deleted [number] this morning."
5. "Your 'personalized' outreach isn't personalized. It's mail merge with better fonts."

**STOLEN THOUGHT:**
6. "Every GTM leader in MENA knows [truth]. Nobody says it in the room."
7. "Your SDR team isn't underperforming. Your signal detection is broken."

**SPECIFICITY:**
8. "[Number] qualified meetings in [days]. No SDR team. Here's the system."
9. "We went from [bad metric] to [good metric] in [timeframe]. One change."

**STORY ENTRY:**
10. "The deal was dead. Then we caught a signal nobody else saw."

**TIMELINESS:**
11. "[Trend/tool/event] just shifted [GTM motion]. Most teams will miss this for 6 months."

**COMPARISON (Old Way / New Way):**
12. "Old way: [painful process + bad metric]. New way: [signal approach + good metric]."
13. "Same campaign. Same list. Same offer. One signal-based. Here's the difference."

**SYSTEM REVEAL:**
14. "The exact signal stack behind [result]. Every tool. Every step. Every number."
15. "Here's how we detect buying intent before the prospect knows they're buying."

### Step 2: Write Post

Apply shared formatting rules. Match voice samples. Include all mandatory elements (disagreement trigger, proof density, pinned comment).

### Step 3: Write Pinned Comment

Bonus value that didn't fit the post. A tool recommendation, an expanded step, or a resource link.

### Step 4: Quality Gate

Run full checklist from linkedin-shared-rules.md. Fix any failure before output.
