# Content Systems

Systematic content production framework for founders to build authority and audience without constant hustle.

## Core Principle

**Content is your moat.** In MENA markets where relationships drive business, consistent content moves you from "founder I know" to "founder I follow."

The system: 1 core idea → 7 formats → 30 pieces/month → $30K+ MRR

## Framework 1: Matt Gray — Content GPS

### The Problem: Content Without Direction

Most founders create random content (sporadic LinkedIn posts). This burns out fast and builds no moat.

Content GPS gives you a map: Clear direction + waypoints + destination.

### The 3-Tier Content GPS Model

**TIER 1: Your North Star Idea**
- One core belief you want to spread
- Example: "MENA founders don't need Silicon Valley GTM; they need founder-led sales"
- Example: "The consultant's playbook is killing your unit economics"
- Example: "Language is your competitive advantage, not liability"

This is your North Star. All content points toward this belief.

**TIER 2: Your Content Pillars (3-4 categories)**

Each pillar is a subcategory that supports your North Star.

Example North Star: "MENA GTM is fundamentally different from US GTM"

Pillar 1: Founder Psychology (Founder behavior, mentality, decision-making)
Pillar 2: Market Dynamics (Gulf markets, relationship selling, pricing psychology)
Pillar 3: Execution Framework (3-Day Launch, founder-led outbound, community building)
Pillar 4: Common Mistakes (Hiring too fast, copying US SaaS, losing to relationships)

**TIER 3: Content Themes (Weekly topics)**

Each week, you pick one theme from your pillars.

Example weekly themes:
- Week 1: "Why hiring a VP of Sales kills MENA startups" (Pillar 1 + Pillar 4)
- Week 2: "The real reason your cold email gets no response in Gulf markets" (Pillar 2)
- Week 3: "How to run a founder-led sales campaign in 72 hours" (Pillar 3)
- Week 4: "The pricing mistake that costs you 50% of revenue" (Pillar 2 + Pillar 4)

### Content GPS Map Template

```

[One core belief that drives all your content]

PILLAR 1: [Category + 2-3 sentence description]
PILLAR 2: [Category + 2-3 sentence description]
PILLAR 3: [Category + 2-3 sentence description]
PILLAR 4: [Category + 2-3 sentence description]

12-MONTH THEME CALENDAR:
Q1: [4 weekly themes that build on each other]
Q2: [4 weekly themes]
Q3: [4 weekly themes]
Q4: [4 weekly themes]
```

## Framework 2: Matt Gray — 7 Core Systems + 30-in-30

### System 1: The Evergreen Content Piece

One long-form essay that never expires and serves as your content hub.

Examples:
- "The MENA GTM Playbook: Everything I've Learned from 50 Founder Exits"
- "The Founder-Led Sales Handbook: How to Get 10 Customers in 30 Days"
- "The 3-Day Launch Formula: From Idea to First Paying Customer"

**How to write:**
- 2,000-3,000 words
- Actionable (readers can apply it immediately)
- Specific to MENA (data, examples, case studies from the region)
- Updated every 3 months with new learnings
- Gated (email capture) OR published free (audience building)

**Repurposing evergreen piece:**
- Evergreen essay → 10 LinkedIn posts (one insight per post)
- Evergreen essay → 1 email sequence (one section per email)
- Evergreen essay → 1 YouTube video (scripted from essay)
- Evergreen essay → 3 Twitter threads

### System 2: Weekly Insights (The Atomic Idea)

One small, specific insight published weekly.

Examples:
- "MENA founders convert at 2x rate when you ask in their native language"
- "The #1 reason your GTM fails: You're trying to hire instead of lead"
- "Your ICP isn't broken. Your positioning is."

**Format:** 1-2 paragraph post with 1 data point or story.
**Repurpose into:** 1 email, 1 LinkedIn post, 1 repost (next week)

### System 3: Customer Stories (Proof)

Real examples of founders you've helped.

**Format:**
- Before: [Situation] (1-2 sentences)
- Problem: [What was broken] (1-2 sentences)
- Intervention: [What you taught them] (1-2 sentences)
- After: [Results with numbers] (1-2 sentences)
- Lesson: [What founders should learn] (1 sentence)

**Repurpose:**
- 1 LinkedIn post
- 1 email (to similar founder segment)
- 1 case study page
- 1 testimonial for sales page
- 1 social proof graphic

**MENA Adaptation:** Use founder names + photos (relationship trust > anonymity)

### System 4: Educational Content (Teaching)

Teach a skill your audience needs.

Examples:
- "How to Run a 3-Day Campaign (Video Tutorial)"
- "The GTM Scorecard: Step-by-Step Application"
- "How to Write an ICP Document (Detailed Guide)"

**Format:** Video, guide, or email sequence teaching one skill end-to-end.

**Repurpose:**
- 1 YouTube video
- 1 long-form blog post
- 1 email sequence (5 emails, one per step)
- 1 LinkedIn carousel (one step per slide)
- 1 checklists/template

### System 5: Contrarian Takes (Hook Points)

Belief-breaking statements that start conversations.

Examples:
- "The founder-led sales era isn't coming. It's already here."
- "Your SaaS playbook is obsolete in MENA markets."
- "The worst thing that happened to startup culture was trying to be like America."

**Format:** Provocative statement + 2-3 supporting points + invitation for debate.

**Repurpose:**
- 1 LinkedIn post (maximum engagement)
- 1 email subject line
- 1 podcast clip
- 1 community discussion starter

### System 6: Pattern Recognition (Deep Insights)

Share patterns you've noticed across 10+ customers.

Examples:
- "The 3 reasons MENA founders fail in GTM (data from 50 exits)"
- "Pattern 1: [description]"
- "Pattern 2: [description]"
- "Pattern 3: [description]"

**Format:** Data + pattern naming + actionable insight.

**Repurpose:**
- 1 LinkedIn post
- 1 email
- 1 blog post (expanded)
- 1 podcast episode (discussed)

### System 7: Community Engagement (Feedback Loop)

Ask questions that get your audience thinking.

Examples:
- "What's the biggest blocker you hit when hiring your first salesperson?"
- "How much does a 'good' GTM person actually cost in the Gulf?"
- "What would you do if you had to launch profitably in 30 days?"

**Format:** Genuine question + context + invitation for response.

**Repurpose:**
- 1 LinkedIn post (Q&A format)
- 1 email (to community)
- 1 Discord/group chat discussion
- Data compilation (aggregate answers, publish insights)

### 30-in-30: Batch Creation System

Create 30 pieces of content in 30 days to build early audience momentum.

**Week 1: Foundation (5 pieces)**
- Write evergreen essay (draft)
- Record 4 micro-videos (phone camera is fine)

**Week 2: Leverage (7 pieces)**
- Publish evergreen essay as blog post
- Create 10 LinkedIn posts from essay insights
- Record email sequence (5 emails, 1 piece)

**Week 3: Teach (8 pieces)**
- Record educational video (YouTube)
- Break into 5-part email sequence
- LinkedIn carousel (5 slides)
- Twitter thread

**Week 4: Proof + Engagement (10 pieces)**
- Collect 2-3 customer stories
- Create 2 case study posts (LinkedIn + blog)
- Create 4 contrarian takes (LinkedIn)
- Create 2 community questions (LinkedIn + Discord)
- Share 2 pattern recognitions

**Total: 30 pieces published across LinkedIn, email, blog, YouTube, Twitter**

## Framework 3: Brendan Kane — Hook Points + Content Waterfall

### The 5 Hook Points Applied to Content

Apply Kane's 5 hook formulas to each content piece:

**1. Contrarian Take**
- "Everything founders know about GTM is wrong"
- "MENA founders are 10x better at [X] than US founders"
- "Your expensive GTM consultant is the problem, not the solution"

**2. Data Disruption**
- "73% of MENA founders fail because..."
- "The average founder wastes $50K on hiring wrong"
- "Founders who use founder-led sales close 5x faster"

**3. Behind-the-Curtain**
- "Here's what happens inside a closed GTM call"
- "The real email sequence that got this founder 10 customers"
- "What I actually charge and why"

**4. Challenge/Dare**
- "I dare you to do founder-led outreach for 72 hours"
- "Can you validate your idea with 5 interviews in one day?"
- "Get one customer commitment by Friday"

**5. Story Hook**
- "Last week a Dubai founder told me..."
- "I was about to quit when this happened..."
- "This mistake cost one founder $100K"

**Apply to every piece:** Does your post have ONE hook point? If not, add it.

### The Content Waterfall: One Core Idea → 7 Formats

One piece of core content gets repurposed into 7 different formats (for different platforms and consumption preferences).

**Core Idea:** "The 3-Day GTM Sprint Formula"

**Format 1: Long-Form Essay (Blog)**
- 2,500 words
- All details, frameworks, templates
- SEO-optimized
- Platform: Blog, Medium

**Format 2: Video Tutorial (YouTube)**
- 15 minutes
- Screen recorded walkthrough
- Chapters: Overview → Framework → Examples → Application
- Platform: YouTube

**Format 3: Email Sequence (Email)**
- 5 emails, one per day
- Email 1: "Here's why your GTM is broken"
- Email 2: "The 3-Day framework (part 1)"
- Email 3: "The 3-Day framework (part 2)"
- Email 4: "Real example: How founder X used it"
- Email 5: "Get started today: Your first 72 hours"
- Platform: Email

**Format 4: LinkedIn Carousel (LinkedIn)**
- 5 slides, one per swipe
- Visual + text
- Slide 1: Hook ("Your GTM is broken...")
- Slides 2-4: Framework steps
- Slide 5: CTA
- Platform: LinkedIn

**Format 5: Twitter Thread (Twitter/X)**
- 10-15 tweets, 1 thread
- Conversational tone
- Each tweet has 1 insight
- Link to blog/video in last tweet
- Platform: Twitter

**Format 6: Short-Form Video (TikTok/Reels)**
- 15-30 second clips
- 3-5 videos per core idea
- Video 1: Problem
- Video 2-4: Solution steps
- Video 5: Result/CTA
- Platform: TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts

**Format 7: Podcast Episode (Audio)**
- 25-40 minutes
- Solo recording, scripted from essay
- Audio on Spotify, Apple Podcasts
- Transcribed and published as blog post
- Platform: Spotify, Apple Podcasts

**Waterfall Production Workflow:**

```
Monday: Write 2,000-word essay
Tuesday: Record 15-min video from essay
Wednesday: Create 5-email sequence from essay
Thursday: Design 5-slide LinkedIn carousel
Friday: Write 10-tweet thread
Saturday: Record 5x 15-second Reels clips
Sunday: Record 30-min podcast episode

Result: 1 core idea
        7 different formats
        30+ total pieces (if you repost across channels)
        Reaches 7 different audience segments
```

## Framework 4: Alex Hormozi — Content-to-Paid Pipeline (12 Months)

### Building the Funnel: Free → Product → Premium

Content alone doesn't make money. The Content-to-Paid Pipeline converts content audience into paying customers.

**Phase 1: Months 1-3 (Audience Building)**
- Publish daily content (via waterfall system)
- Goal: 5,000 followers/email subscribers
- Focus: Volume + consistency (hook point every post)
- Monetization: NONE (seed audience)

**Phase 2: Months 4-6 (Lead Generation)**
- Publish 5x/week (same waterfall, higher frequency)
- Create lead magnet (free guide, checklist, template)
- Build email list (goal: 1,000+ emails)
- Focus: Building trust + email capture
- Monetization: Email list (no product yet)

**Phase 3: Months 7-9 (Low-Ticket Product)**
- Launch $97-197 product (course, template bundle, mini-course)
- Email list promotion (3-5 emails)
- Content marketing (20% of posts mention product)
- Goal: $1-2K MRR
- Focus: Proof of concept + social proof collection

**Phase 4: Months 10-12 (High-Ticket + Continuity)**
- Launch $2-5K product (consulting, done-with-you program)
- Monthly webinar to email list (product pitch)
- Content focuses on specific customer segment
- Launch continuity product ($500-1,000/month)
- Goal: $5-10K MRR by month 12

### Timeline Example

**Month 1-3: Daily LinkedIn posts**
- 1 post/day = 90 posts
- 1 long-form essay (1,000 words)
- Goal: 3,000 followers

**Month 4-6: Add email list**
- Continue daily LinkedIn
- Create lead magnet (free guide: "The 3-Day GTM Sprint")
- Email sequence (5 emails explaining GTM)
- Goal: 1,000 email subscribers

**Month 7-9: Launch low-ticket product**
- Continue daily content
- Launch "$97 GTM Sprint Template Bundle"
- 3-email launch sequence
- 20% of posts mention template
- Goal: 50+ customers = $4,850 MRR

**Month 10-12: Build high-ticket**
- Continue content + email
- Launch "$3K 90-Day GTM Program" (4 cohorts of 5 = 20 customers)
- Monthly webinar
- Launch "$500/month Founder Community"
- Goal: $10K+ MRR

## Framework 5: Tibo — Build-in-Public Strategy

### The Audience is Your Moat

In MENA especially, building publicly transforms you from expert to familiar, from transactional to trusted.

**Build-in-Public Layers:**

**Layer 1: Progress Updates (Weekly)**
- Share what you're working on
- Share the numbers (revenue, customers, challenges)
- Share failures (not just wins)

Example:
"Week 14 update: 42 founders in the community, $6.2K MRR, but lost 2 customers this week because I wasn't responsive enough. Learning: community needs more direct access to me, not just Slack. Adding weekly 1:1 office hours."

**Layer 2: Decision-Making (Real-time)**
- Ask audience for input on key decisions
- Share pro/con of each option
- Implement decision + report results

Example:
"Should I charge $297 or $497 for the 90-day program? Here's my thinking... What would you do?"

**Layer 3: Teaching Through Doing (Async)**
- Document your process as you do the work
- Don't wait for perfection—share in progress
- Audience learns + helps you improve

Example:
"I'm rebuilding the training curriculum. Here's what I added today [3 screenshots]. What do you think is missing?"

**Layer 4: Vulnerability (Strategic)**
- Share struggles, not just wins
- Show failures and pivots
- Admit when you're wrong

Example:
"My GTM strategy doesn't work for SaaS founders (it's too relationship-heavy). I'm pivoting to focus on B2B services founders instead. Here's what I got wrong..."

### MENA-Specific Build-in-Public

**WhatsApp > Blog for real-time updates:**
- Daily WhatsApp update (60 seconds, voice message)
- Weekly community call (discuss builds + get feedback)
- Monthly celebration of student wins (public acknowledgment)

**Bilingual matters:**
- Arabic updates for Arabic-speaking community
- English updates for international reach
- Don't translate—create distinct content per language

**Community-first metric:**
- Not followers, but "people who know your voice"
- Not vanity, but referral rate
- Success: Founder gets customers through community referrals
