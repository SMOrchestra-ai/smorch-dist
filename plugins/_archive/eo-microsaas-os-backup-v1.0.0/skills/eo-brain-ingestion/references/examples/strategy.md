# Strategy - EO Oasis

## Recommended Strategy Path
Replicate & Localize

## Path Description
Take a proven global model (product launch/discovery platform) and deeply localize it for MENA Arabic-speaking founders. Not a translation job, a structural adaptation of trust mechanics, payment flows, community dynamics, and distribution channels for the region.

## Backup Path
Boring Micro-SaaS (if localization proves insufficient differentiation, fall back to a simpler tool-based approach)

## Founder Archetype
The Closer - Revenue-first founder who validates by selling. Does not build until someone pays.

## Archetype Strengths
- Validates through revenue, not surveys
- Sales skills de-risk go-to-market
- Closes deals before product is fully built
- Network effects from existing relationships

## Archetype Risks
- May over-index on sales and under-invest in product
- Risk of selling vaporware if not disciplined
- Must hire product/technical talent early to complement sales DNA

## 90-Day Roadmap

### Month 1: Research + Select + Map + Design Partners
- Finalize reference model analysis (Product Hunt, AppSumo, BetaList adaptations)
- Map MENA-specific requirements (trust layers, Arabic UX, payment methods)
- Recruit 3 design partner founders (one per market: Dubai, Amman, Cairo)
- Validate pricing with 10+ founders willing to commit

### Month 2: Build + Localize + Beta
- Build MVP (Next.js + Supabase + GHL + n8n)
- Implement Arabic-first UX with credibility scoring system
- Onboard design partners for beta testing
- Iterate based on weekly founder feedback sessions

### Month 3: Launch + Scale to 20 Customers + Iterate
- Public launch across 4 markets simultaneously
- Activate all 5 distribution channels (LinkedIn, WhatsApp, YouTube, Telegram, Events)
- Target: 20 paying customers by end of Month 3
- Weekly iteration cycles based on usage data and founder feedback

## Strategy Score
97/100 - LAUNCH READY

## MENA Execution Advantage
20 years of commercial selling in all 4 target markets plus live Arabic-speaking distribution channel (200+ waitlist, 820+ WhatsApp community). This is not "I know the region" positioning, it is paid commercial proof.

## Biggest Challenge
Regional fragmentation across 4 markets with different regulations, payment systems, and cultural nuances. Mitigation: launch simultaneously but with market-specific onboarding flows and localized community channels per country.
