# Engagement-Before-Outreach Playbook

## Core Principle
Cold outreach on LinkedIn has a ~5% CR acceptance rate. Engagement-before-outreach (visible familiarity) pushes that to 15-25% in our testing. The cost is 3-5 days of patience. The return is 3-5x better conversion.

## The Engagement Ladder

```
Level 0: Complete stranger (cold CR = 3-5% accept)
  ↓ Like their post
Level 1: Name recognition (they see your name in notifications)
  ↓ Thoughtful comment on their post
Level 2: Visible contributor (they read your perspective)
  ↓ Reply to their comment on someone else's post
Level 3: Familiar voice (multiple touchpoints registered)
  ↓ Send CR with note referencing shared context
Level 4: Connected (CR accepted = 15-25% from Level 3)
  ↓ Value-add message (not pitch)
Level 5: Conversation open (they respond to your message)
```

The goal is NOT to rush from Level 0 to Level 5. The goal is to make each step feel natural so the CR doesn't feel cold when it arrives.

## Engagement Types (Ordered by Signal Strength)

### 1. Thoughtful Comment (Highest Value)
A substantive comment that adds perspective, data, or experience to their post.

**Good comment patterns:**
- "We saw something similar at [company]. The thing that changed the outcome was [specific insight]."
- "Interesting take. One thing I'd add from working with [similar companies]: [observation]."
- "This is exactly the challenge with [specific problem]. Have you found that [specific tactic] helps?"

**Bad comment patterns (never do these):**
- "Great post!" / "Love this!" / "So true!" (zero value, looks automated)
- "Interesting, I just posted about this too! Check it out: [link]" (hijacking)
- "We solve this exact problem! DM me." (selling in comments = reputation damage)

### 2. Reaction + View (Low Effort, Cumulative Value)
Like their post + view their profile. They get TWO notifications: "[Name] liked your post" and "[Name] viewed your profile."

Stack this across 2-3 posts over a week = they see your name repeatedly without you writing anything.

### 3. Reshare with Commentary (High Value but Use Sparingly)
Reshare their post with a substantive add-on. This puts their content in YOUR network, which:
- Gives them exposure (reciprocity trigger)
- Shows your network you engage with relevant voices
- Creates a strong notification signal

Only do this for content you genuinely find valuable. Forced reshares look worse than no engagement.

### 4. Reply to Their Comment on Others' Posts
Find posts where your prospect commented and add a reply to THEIR comment (not the original post). This is the least obvious engagement tactic but creates strong familiarity because:
- It shows you read what they write, not just their posts
- The notification says "[Name] replied to your comment" - different from standard post notifications
- It positions you as part of the same conversation circle

## Timing Protocol

### Standard Engagement Sequence (5-Day)
```
Day 1: Like their most recent relevant post
Day 2: View their profile (generates profile view notification)
Day 3: Leave a thoughtful comment on a different post
Day 4: Like or react to one more piece of their content
Day 5: Send CR with note: "Been following your thinking on [topic]. [One-sentence wedge]. Worth connecting?"
```

### Accelerated Sequence (3-Day - for Tier 1 signals only)
```
Day 1: Thoughtful comment on their most signal-rich post
Day 2: Like another post + view profile
Day 3: Send CR referencing the post you commented on
```

### MENA-Adjusted Sequence (7-Day - higher trust requirement)
```
Day 1: Like post + profile view
Day 2: Thoughtful comment (English or matching their post language)
Day 3: Engage with their comment elsewhere
Day 4-5: Like 1-2 more posts
Day 6: Check for mutual connections - ask for context/intro if available
Day 7: Send CR with note referencing engagement + mutual connection if applicable
```

## CR Note Templates (Post-Engagement)

### After Commenting on Their Content
```
[First name], your post about [specific topic] resonated - especially [specific point].

Building something in this space for [region/industry]. Thought it's worth connecting.

- [Your name]
```
(Under 300 characters)

### After Multiple Engagements
```
[First name], been following your perspective on [topic]. Sharp thinking.

Working on [brief one-liner about what you do] for [target market]. Happy to exchange notes.
```

### MENA with Mutual Connection
```
[First name], noticed we both know [mutual connection name]. Your work at [company] in [specific area] caught my attention.

Would be great to connect.
```

### Arabic CR Note
```
[الاسم الأول]، متابع لمحتواك عن [الموضوع]. نقاط ممتازة.

أعمل على [وصف مختصر] في سوق الخليج. يسعدني التواصل.
```

## Engagement DO NOTs

1. **Don't engage with EVERY post in sequence** - looks automated
2. **Don't like posts from 3 months ago** - looks like stalking
3. **Don't comment on personal/family posts** - stay professional
4. **Don't engage during Ramadan evening hours with sales intent** - engage genuinely or not at all
5. **Don't mass-engage 20 prospects in one sitting** - LinkedIn detects patterns
6. **Don't engage with competitor's prospects while they're in active deal** - bad form in MENA
7. **Don't use engagement as an excuse to pitch in comments** - ever

## Tracking Engagement Sequences

For each active engagement sequence:
```
Prospect: [Name]
Start date: [date]
Day 1 action: [what you did] ✓/pending
Day 2 action: [what you did] ✓/pending
Day 3 action: [what you did] ✓/pending
CR sent: [date] / pending
CR accepted: yes/no/pending
First message sent: [date] / pending
Response received: yes/no/pending
```

Maintain a running list of max 10-15 active engagement sequences. More than that becomes unmanageable and the quality drops.

## Measuring Engagement ROI

Track monthly:
- CRs sent with prior engagement vs cold CRs
- Acceptance rate: engagement-warmed vs cold
- Response rate to first message: warmed vs cold
- Meetings booked from each approach
- Time investment per approach (engagement takes more time but converts better)

Expected benchmarks:
- Cold CR acceptance: 3-8%
- Engagement-warmed CR acceptance: 15-25%
- Cold message response: 2-5%
- Warmed message response: 10-20%
