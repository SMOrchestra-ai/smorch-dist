# Content Analysis Framework

## Purpose
Systematic approach to analyzing someone's LinkedIn content to extract actionable intelligence for signal-based outreach.

## The Content Signal Hierarchy

Not all content is equal as an outreach signal. Ranked by signal strength:

### Tier 1 - Direct Buying Signals (Act within 48 hours)
- **Pain posts**: Complaints about current tools, processes, or results
  - Pattern: "We've been struggling with...", "Anyone else frustrated by...", "Looking for alternatives to..."
  - Action: Reference specific pain in outreach
- **Evaluation posts**: Asking for recommendations or comparisons
  - Pattern: "What CRM do you use?", "Recommendations for...", "Has anyone tried..."
  - Action: Offer expertise/benchmark, not a pitch
- **Budget/hiring posts**: Announcing team growth or new initiatives
  - Pattern: "Excited to announce we're building...", "Hiring for...", "Just got approved for..."
  - Action: Reference their growth trajectory

### Tier 2 - Topic Alignment Signals (Engage first, outreach in 3-5 days)
- **Industry commentary**: Opinions on market trends relevant to your offer
  - Pattern: Posts about industry challenges you solve
  - Action: Add value in comments, then CR with reference
- **How-we-did-it posts**: Sharing internal processes or results
  - Pattern: "Here's how our team handles...", "We increased X by Y%..."
  - Action: Compliment specific insight, offer adjacent benchmark
- **Event/conference posts**: Attending or speaking at relevant events
  - Pattern: "Just attended...", "Speaking at...", "Key takeaways from..."
  - Action: Reference shared interest in event topic

### Tier 3 - Context Signals (Use for personalization, no urgency)
- **Personal milestones**: Work anniversaries, promotions, awards
  - Pattern: "Grateful for X years at...", "Excited to share..."
  - Action: Genuine congratulation, no pitch attached
- **Reshares**: What content do they amplify? Reveals interests and network
  - Pattern: Reshares of thought leaders, industry reports, company news
  - Action: Use to understand their information diet
- **Company promotions**: Sharing company news, product launches
  - Pattern: Corporate content, employer branding
  - Action: Low signal for outreach, but useful for company intel

### Noise (Skip - no signal value)
- Motivational quotes without personal context
- Generic "happy [holiday]" posts
- Auto-generated LinkedIn anniversary posts
- Engagement bait polls with no substance

## Content Analysis Checklist

For each person's last 10 posts, answer:

1. **Frequency**: How often do they post? (daily = thought leader, monthly = occasional, never = lurker)
2. **Originality**: Original thoughts vs reshares vs company content?
3. **Depth**: Short takes vs long-form? Surface level vs detailed?
4. **Topics**: What 2-3 themes dominate? Do they align with your offer?
5. **Engagement**: Do their posts get reactions? Comments? Or crickets?
6. **Voice**: Formal corporate vs casual personal? Educational vs opinionated?
7. **Language**: English only? Arabic only? Mixed? Code-switching patterns?
8. **Network**: Who comments on their posts? (peers, vendors, juniors?)
9. **Recency**: When was their last post? (>30 days = declining activity)
10. **Signal density**: How many Tier 1 or Tier 2 signals in last 10 posts?

## Content-to-Outreach Translation

### The Formula
```
Content signal + Your relevance + Low-friction CTA = Conversation permission
```

### Examples

**Their post**: "We tried 3 different outreach tools this quarter and none of them worked for the Gulf market."
**Translation**: Pain signal + MENA-specific problem + tool evaluation
**Outreach angle**: "Your post about outreach tools in the Gulf resonated. We cracked a similar problem for [specific client type]. Happy to share the approach if useful."

**Their post**: "Just promoted Sarah to Head of RevOps. Our revenue operations team is finally getting the investment it needs."
**Translation**: Budget signal + new team + ops investment
**Outreach angle**: "Congrats on building out the RevOps function. When teams hit that inflection point, the ops stack usually becomes the bottleneck. Is that the case for you?"

**Their post**: Reshared an article about "AI in B2B sales" with comment "This is the future."
**Translation**: Topic interest + AI alignment + thought leadership aspiration
**Outreach angle**: Reference the article + your specific experience implementing what the article discusses

## Comment Analysis (Advanced)

Sometimes the comments section reveals more than the post itself.

### What to look for in comments:
- **Their comments on others' posts**: Shows what they care about when not performing
- **Who comments on their posts**: Maps their network and influence
- **Comment depth**: One-word reactions vs thoughtful replies
- **Vendor mentions in comments**: Are they talking to your competitors?
- **Question patterns**: Questions they ask reveal knowledge gaps

### How to use comment intel:
- If they comment on a competitor's post: they're evaluating. Outreach NOW
- If they ask detailed questions: they're researching. Offer expertise
- If they thank someone for advice: identify who influences them
- If industry peers comment: these peers might also be prospects

## Batch Content Analysis (for Mode E)

When analyzing content for multiple prospects:

1. Open each profile's activity feed
2. Quick scan: look for any Tier 1 signal in last 5 posts
3. If Tier 1 found: flag for immediate outreach, capture the hook
4. If only Tier 2: flag for engagement-first sequence
5. If no signals: capture headline + last post topic as fallback personalization
6. Time budget: 2-3 minutes per person for quick scan
