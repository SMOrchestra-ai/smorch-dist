## DM Sequence Rules

**Cold DM best practices:**
1. **Personalization must be VISIBLE** — The {{signal}} should be something they said/did, not guessed
2. **Keep under 300 characters** — LinkedIn mobile view cuts off long messages
3. **No hype language** — "Hey this might interest you" works better than "This is life-changing"
4. **One ask only** — Just register. Don't ask to connect, don't ask for a call, don't ask for feedback
5. **Signature should be minimal** — First name only is fine; full title feels corporate

**What kills cold DMs:**
- "I hope this email finds you well" (generic, kills trust)
- Multiple CTAs (confusing signal)
- No personalization (obvious it's a template)
- Too long (gets cut off, seems desperate)
- Asking for something before giving value

## Engagement After DM

**If they respond to your DM:**
- Respond within 1-2 hours (shows personal attention)
- If they have a question: answer directly (don't push them to register harder)
- If they register: "Great, see you Thursday [time]. Quick Q&A after so bring a question"
- If they don't register but engage: "No pressure, but link is here if you want: [LINK]"

**Track metrics:**
- Total DMs sent: X
- Positive responses: Y (% click-through)
- Registrations from DMs: Z
- Cost per registration: $0 (you're doing outreach)

## Email List Management

**Before sending:**
- Segment email list (don't send to full list, segment by relevance)
- Warm segment: 1,200-1,500 people (have engaged with you, bought from you, or are leads)
- Cold segment: everyone else (lower expect open rates)

**Optimal send times (local timezone):**
- Email 1 (registration push): 10am (3-5 days before)
- Email 2 (urgency): 11am day before (11am)
- Email 3 (post-event): 2pm same day OR 10am next day

**A/B testing:**
- Split email 1 subject line 50/50 (send both versions to half your list)
- Use winning subject line for follow-up (post-event email 3)
- Track open rate and click rate for each version
